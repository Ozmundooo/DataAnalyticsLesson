Asking the Right Questions

Before creating a question that will help with predicting how your data will be, you must ask yourself who your target audience is and is the data sufficient to give you both a question and a probable answer.

Steps to asking the right questions:

  • Frame the question as what question would I like to have an answer to, not what is the answer I'm looking for?
  • Frame your question in more specific terms?
  • Do you have the capabilities to answer your question?

Unlocking the Data Within

Determining what data to is more complicated than it seems, the two major issues that can occur with that is complication in finding uses for the datasets available and having unclean data

Common causes of errors:

The “Fat Finger” Problem – When someone inputting data misinputs a character or number Ex. 100000 -> 1000000

Conventions – Sometimes accounting and personal convention get incorporated in the formulas of data which leads to what looks to be altered results that can seem worrisome. For example, if a company does their pay outs every two weeks, the profits of the company will see a dip every two weeks.

Mistakes in formulas – As seen in the previous examples using excel, formulas were used to find the values of the Mean, Median and Mode, as easy as it was to input those formulas it is just as easy to misinput them

Migration Issues – When data is shifted to a different server/database management system, this causes the data to be stored in a different fashion which will may not allow some of the data to compile properly resulting in skewed results


Sampling

A process conducted to obtain a very summarized average of a small group to help make predictions on a larger scale

Sampling is often done out of necessity, it is used for voting which will not provide a proper summarization for a country but can provide insight on how a company is doing on a smaller scale, for example conduction surveys done by a select few customers.

Cherry Picking

Selective use of data to support one’s position, while ignoring other data that tends to counter one’s opinion.

Tips regarding Cherry-Picking

  • Verify the data
  • Think about data selection
  • Avoid promoting through statistics
  • Ensure reliability of data

Forcasting

Forecasting is making predictions based on data from the past and present


The purpose of forecasting is not to truly predict the future but rather reduce the number of likely outcomes


Issues that can arise while Forecasting

  • Different factors
  • Random chance vs. Actual effect
